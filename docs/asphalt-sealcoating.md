## Why Sealcoating Is Essential for Your Asphalt

Asphalt naturally breaks down over time due to sunlight, moisture, chemicals, and vehicle traffic. Sealcoating acts as a durable shield that:

- Prevents moisture penetration and reduces cracking
- Protects against oxidation and fading
- Resists oil, gas, salt, and chemical spills
- Extends asphalt lifespan by 5 to 10 plus years
- Enhances curb appeal with a clean, black finish
- Reduces long-term repair and maintenance costs

Sealcoating is one of the most effective and affordable ways to protect your pavement investment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is asphalt sealcoating?

Sealcoating is the process of applying a protective coating over asphalt surfaces to safeguard them from weather damage, traffic wear, UV rays, oil spills, and moisture.

### Why is sealcoating important?

Sealcoating protects the surface, slows deterioration, prevents cracking, and extends the lifespan of your pavement.

### How often should asphalt be sealcoated?

Most asphalt surfaces should be sealcoated every 2 to 3 years, depending on traffic volume, climate, and wear.

### What type of sealcoat do you use?

We use commercial-grade, highly durable asphalt emulsions and coal-tar-based sealers when permitted.

### Does sealcoating make asphalt look new again?

Yes. Sealcoating restores a deep black, smooth finish that dramatically improves curb appeal.
